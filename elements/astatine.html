Astatine

Astatine, symbol At, is a highly radioactive element with atomic number 85. It is a member of the halogen group and is extremely rare, with only trace amounts found in nature.

General Properties

  • Symbol: At
  • Atomic Number: 85
  • Atomic Mass: 210
  • Density: 6.0 g/cm³ (estimated)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 302 °C
  • Boiling Point: 337 °C (estimated)

Importance in the Universe

Astatine is one of the rarest naturally occurring elements, formed in trace amounts through the decay of heavier elements. It is of interest in scientific research, especially in the fields of radiochemistry and medicine.

Key Applications

  • Radiotherapy: Astatine-211 is used in targeted alpha-particle cancer therapy due to its radioactivity, offering potential for cancer treatment.
  • Scientific Research: Astatine is used in nuclear physics and chemistry experiments, although its rarity limits its use in practical applications.

Did You Know?

Astatine is so rare that it is estimated that less than 1 gram of natural astatine exists on Earth at any given time.

Isotopes of Astatine

Astatine has several isotopes, with Astatine-210 and Astatine-211 being the most notable. Astatine-211 is used in radiotherapy due to its ability to emit alpha particles, which can damage cancerous cells.

Chemical Properties

Astatine behaves similarly to iodine and other halogens. However, due to its radioactivity, it is far less studied and harder to handle than other elements in its group. It forms compounds such as Astatine(V) oxide and Astatine salts.
