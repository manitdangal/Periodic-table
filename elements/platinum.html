Platinum

Platinum, symbol Pt, is a transition metal with atomic number 78. Known for its exceptional resistance to corrosion and attractive silvery-white appearance, platinum is highly valued in industrial, medical, and decorative applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Pt
  • Atomic Number: 78
  • Atomic Mass: 195.08
  • Density: 21.45 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,768 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,825 °C

Importance in the Universe

Platinum is a rare element formed in supernova explosions. On Earth, it is found in alluvial deposits and is used for its catalytic properties and beauty.

Key Applications

  • Jewelry: Highly sought after for its beauty and resistance to tarnish.
  • Catalysis: Used in catalytic converters and chemical reactions for its ability to speed up processes without being consumed.
  • Medical Devices: Platinum is used in pacemakers and other implants due to its biocompatibility.

Did You Know?

Platinum's name comes from the Spanish term "platina," meaning "little silver."

Isotopes of Platinum

Platinum has six naturally occurring isotopes, including Platinum-195, which is used in nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy.

Chemical Properties

Platinum is highly resistant to oxidation and corrosion, even at high temperatures. It forms compounds such as Platinum(II) chloride, widely used in industrial and laboratory settings.
