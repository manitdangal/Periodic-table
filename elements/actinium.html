Actinium

Actinium, symbol Ac, is a highly radioactive element with atomic number 89. It is one of the rarest naturally occurring elements and was the first non-primordial radioactive element to be isolated.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ac
  • Atomic Number: 89
  • Atomic Mass: 227
  • Density: 2.37 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,050 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,200 °C (estimated)

Importance in the Universe

Actinium is an important element in the study of radioactive decay and nuclear processes. It was used in early radiotherapy but has since been largely replaced by safer alternatives.

Key Applications

  • Radiotherapy: Actinium-227 is used in targeted alpha-particle cancer therapies.
  • Scientific Research: Actinium is used as a neutron source in research on nuclear fission and radiation effects.

Did You Know?

Actinium glows faintly in the dark due to its radioactive decay.

Isotopes of Actinium

Actinium-227 is the most stable isotope of actinium, with a half-life of 21.7 years. It is a source of alpha particles and has been used in cancer treatment and radiopharmaceuticals.

Chemical Properties

Actinium is highly reactive and forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Actinium(III) chloride and Actinium(III) oxide. It reacts with water and oxygen and is typically stored under oil to prevent oxidation.
