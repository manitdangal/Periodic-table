Carbon

Carbon, symbol C, is the sixth element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 6. It is a non-metal and the basis of all known life, forming the backbone of organic molecules. Carbon exists in several allotropes, including graphite, diamond, and graphene.

General Properties

  • Symbol: C
  • Atomic Number: 6
  • Atomic Mass: 12.01 amu
  • Density: 2.267 g/cm³ (graphite)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: Sublimates at 3642°C (graphite)
  • Boiling Point: Not applicable (sublimation)

Key Applications

Carbon is integral to numerous fields and industries due to its chemical and structural properties:

  • Steel Production: Carbon is used as an alloying element in the production of steel.
  • Energy: Carbon-based fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas are major energy sources.
  • Electronics: Graphene and carbon nanotubes are used in advanced electronics and materials science.
  • Biology: Carbon is the fundamental building block of organic compounds in living organisms.

Did You Know?

Diamonds, a form of carbon, are among the hardest known materials on Earth, while graphite, another form, is soft and slippery, used as a lubricant and in pencils.

Chemical Properties

Carbon is highly versatile and forms countless compounds, including:

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): A greenhouse gas that plays a critical role in Earth's climate and photosynthesis.
  • Carbonates: Found in minerals like limestone and marble.
  • Organic Compounds: Carbon forms the basis of all known organic molecules, including carbohydrates, proteins, and DNA.
