Zirconium

Zirconium, symbol Zr, is the 40th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 40. It is a transition metal known for its strength, resistance to corrosion, and high melting point.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Zr
  • Atomic Number: 40
  • Atomic Mass: 91.224 amu
  • Density: 6.52 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1855°C
  • Boiling Point: 4377°C

Importance in the Universe

Zirconium is rare on Earth but abundant in stars, where it plays a role in the formation of other heavy elements. It is also found in trace amounts in meteorites.

Key Applications

Zirconium has several important applications:

  • Aerospace: Zirconium alloys are used in jet engines and rockets due to their high resistance to heat and corrosion.
  • Nuclear Reactors: Zirconium is used as a cladding material for fuel rods in nuclear reactors because it has a low neutron-capture cross-section.
  • Jewelry: Zirconium is used in the creation of durable and hypoallergenic rings and watches.

Did You Know?

Zirconium was first isolated in 1824 by Swedish chemist Jöns Jacob Berzelius from the mineral zircon.

Isotopes of Zirconium

Zirconium has five stable isotopes, the most common of which is Zirconium-90. It is used in a wide variety of industrial applications due to its chemical properties.

Chemical Properties

Zirconium is highly resistant to corrosion and forms compounds such as:

  • Zirconium Dioxide (ZrO₂): Used in ceramics and as an insulation material in high-temperature applications.
  • Zirconium Tetrachloride (ZrCl₄): A key precursor in the production of zirconium metal.
