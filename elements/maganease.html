Manganese

Manganese, symbol Mn, is the 25th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 25. It is a brittle, silvery-white metal that is widely used in the production of steel and other alloys due to its ability to improve hardness and strength.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Mn
  • Atomic Number: 25
  • Atomic Mass: 54.938045 amu
  • Density: 7.43 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1244°C
  • Boiling Point: 2095°C

Importance in the Universe

Manganese is a rare element in the Earth’s crust but is an important trace element in biological systems. It plays a key role in the formation of stars and is essential for life as a cofactor in enzymes.

Key Applications

Manganese is primarily used in the following industries:

  • Steel Production: Manganese is essential in the production of steel, improving its strength, toughness, and hardness.
  • Batteries: Manganese is used in lithium-ion batteries, particularly in cathodes, for high-performance electric vehicles and other technologies.
  • Fertilizers: Manganese compounds are used in fertilizers to promote plant growth.
  • Chemicals: Manganese compounds are used as catalysts in several chemical processes, including in the production of various chemicals like potassium permanganate.

Did You Know?

Manganese is an essential nutrient in human nutrition, important for bone development, metabolism, and the functioning of enzymes. It is often found in foods such as nuts, legumes, and grains.

Isotopes of Manganese

The most stable isotope of manganese is Manganese-55, which is found in nature. Other isotopes have been produced artificially, some of which are radioactive.

Chemical Properties

Manganese is highly reactive and readily forms compounds with other elements:

  • Manganese Dioxide (MnO₂): Used in dry-cell batteries and as an oxidizing agent in chemical reactions.
  • Manganese Sulfate (MnSO₄): Used in the production of fertilizers and animal feed.
  • Potassium Permanganate (KMnO₄): A strong oxidizing agent used in disinfectants, water treatment, and as a chemical reagent in laboratories.
