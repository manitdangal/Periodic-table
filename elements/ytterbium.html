Ytterbium

Ytterbium, symbol Yb, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 70. It is a soft, silvery-white metal that exhibits unique properties in quantum physics and materials science.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Yb
  • Atomic Number: 70
  • Atomic Mass: 173.04
  • Density: 6.90 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 824 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1,196 °C

Importance in the Universe

Ytterbium is used in quantum physics experiments and is an important component in certain steels and electronics. It is also used in atomic clocks due to its stability.

Key Applications

Ytterbium is used in several key applications:

  • Alloys: Ytterbium is used to improve the properties of stainless steel and other alloys.
  • Lasers: Ytterbium is used in high-power solid-state lasers.
  • Atomic Clocks: Ytterbium atoms are used in some of the most precise atomic clocks.

Did You Know?

Ytterbium is named after Ytterby, a village in Sweden where it was first discovered.

Isotopes of Ytterbium

Ytterbium has seven stable isotopes, with Ytterbium-174 being the most abundant. Its isotopes are used in materials science and nuclear research.

Chemical Properties

Ytterbium commonly forms compounds in the +2 and +3 oxidation states. It reacts slowly with oxygen and water, forming a protective oxide layer.
