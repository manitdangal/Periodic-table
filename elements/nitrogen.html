Nitrogen

Nitrogen, symbol N, is the seventh element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 7. It is a non-metal and is essential for life, making up approximately 78% of Earth's atmosphere. Nitrogen is colorless, odorless, and inert under standard conditions.

General Properties

  • Symbol: N
  • Atomic Number: 7
  • Atomic Mass: 14.007 amu
  • Density: 1.2506 g/L (at 0°C and 1 atm)
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas
  • Melting Point: -210.00°C
  • Boiling Point: -195.79°C

Importance in the Universe

Nitrogen is the most abundant element in the Earth's atmosphere. It plays a key role in the nitrogen cycle, which is crucial for the production of proteins and nucleic acids in living organisms. Nitrogen also exists in stars and nebulae, where it is part of molecular clouds.

Key Applications

Nitrogen has various applications in different fields, some of which include:

  • Ammonia Production: Nitrogen is a key component in the Haber process to produce ammonia, a vital compound in fertilizers.
  • Inert Atmosphere: Nitrogen is often used to create an inert atmosphere for chemical reactions, especially in industries like electronics and food packaging.
  • Medicine: Nitrous oxide, commonly known as laughing gas, is used as an anesthetic and in pain management.
  • Cryogenics: Liquid nitrogen is used for cooling in cryogenics, as well as in the preservation of biological specimens.

Did You Know?

Nitrogen gas (N₂) is extremely stable due to the strong triple bond between the nitrogen atoms, which is why it is inert and does not easily react with other elements under standard conditions.

Chemical Properties

Nitrogen is highly reactive under certain conditions and forms various compounds, such as:

  • Ammonia (NH₃): A key compound in fertilizers, made by combining nitrogen with hydrogen.
  • Nitric Acid (HNO₃): A strong acid used in the production of fertilizers and explosives.
  • Nitrogen Oxides (NOx): Important for environmental chemistry, particularly in air pollution and the formation of smog.
