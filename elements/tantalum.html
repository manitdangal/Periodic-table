Tantalum

Tantalum, symbol Ta, is a transition metal with atomic number 73. It is a rare, hard, and highly corrosion-resistant metal, often used in electronics and specialized engineering applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ta
  • Atomic Number: 73
  • Atomic Mass: 180.95
  • Density: 16.69 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 3,017 °C
  • Boiling Point: 5,458 °C

Importance in the Universe

Tantalum is crucial in modern technology due to its ability to withstand high temperatures and resist corrosion. Its excellent conductivity and durability make it ideal for capacitors and high-performance equipment.

Key Applications

Tantalum is used in several advanced applications:

  • Electronics: Tantalum capacitors are essential components in smartphones, computers, and other electronic devices.
  • Aerospace: Tantalum alloys are used in turbine blades and rocket components for their strength and heat resistance.
  • Medical: Tantalum is biocompatible and is used in surgical implants and medical devices.

Did You Know?

Tantalum was discovered in 1802 and named after the mythological figure Tantalus because of its resistance to absorbing acid, similar to Tantalus’s curse in Greek mythology.

Isotopes of Tantalum

Tantalum has two stable isotopes: Tantalum-181 and Tantalum-180m, with the latter being a rare nuclear isomer studied for its unique properties.

Chemical Properties

Tantalum is highly inert and resistant to corrosion, even in strong acids. It forms compounds such as Tantalum(V) oxide and Tantalum(V) chloride, which are widely used in industrial and electronic applications.
