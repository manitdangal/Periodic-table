Yttrium

Yttrium, symbol Y, is the 39th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 39. It is a soft, silvery-metallic transition metal with various applications in electronics and materials science.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Y
  • Atomic Number: 39
  • Atomic Mass: 88.905 amu
  • Density: 4.472 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1526°C
  • Boiling Point: 3337°C

Importance in the Universe

Yttrium is found in trace amounts in the Earth's crust and is produced in stars. It plays a key role in electronics and materials science.

Key Applications

Yttrium is used in various high-tech and industrial applications:

  • LEDs: Yttrium is used in phosphors for producing red light in LED displays.
  • Superconductors: Yttrium is a key element in the production of high-temperature superconductors.
  • Medical Imaging: Yttrium isotopes are used in cancer treatment, specifically in radioisotope therapy.

Did You Know?

Yttrium was discovered in 1794 by Swedish chemist Carl Gustav Mosander, and it was named after the Swedish village of Ytterby, where the mineral containing it was first discovered.

Isotopes of Yttrium

Yttrium has one stable isotope, Yttrium-89, and several radioactive isotopes used in medical and industrial applications.

Chemical Properties

Yttrium is relatively stable and forms various compounds, including:

  • Yttrium Oxide (Y₂O₃): Used in electronics, ceramics, and as a phosphor in color television tubes.
  • Yttrium Fluoride (YF₃): A compound used in research and manufacturing.
