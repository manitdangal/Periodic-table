Tellurium

Tellurium, symbol Te, is the 52nd element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 52. It is a brittle, silvery-white metalloid with a slight metallic luster. Tellurium is mainly used in alloys and in the production of semiconductor devices.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Te
  • Atomic Number: 52
  • Atomic Mass: 127.60 amu
  • Density: 6.24 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 449.5°C
  • Boiling Point: 988°C

Importance in the Universe

Tellurium is a rare element in the Earth's crust and is considered a metalloid because it shares properties of both metals and non-metals. It is important for a variety of electronic and solar applications.

Key Applications

Tellurium is used in:

  • Semiconductors: Tellurium is used in thermoelectric devices, as it can conduct electricity under certain conditions.
  • Alloys: It is alloyed with other metals to improve their machinability and strength.
  • Solar Cells: Tellurium is used in the manufacture of thin-film solar cells.

Did You Know?

Tellurium compounds are used as catalysts in chemical reactions, especially in the petroleum industry.

Isotopes of Tellurium

Tellurium has eight stable isotopes, with Tellurium-130 and Tellurium-128 being the most abundant.

Chemical Properties

Tellurium forms compounds such as:

  • Tellurium Dioxide (TeO₂): Used in ceramics and glassmaking.
  • Tellurium Hexafluoride (TeF₆): A highly reactive compound used in chemical processes.
