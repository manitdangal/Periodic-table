Xenon

Xenon, symbol Xe, is the 54th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 54. It is a colorless, odorless, and inert noble gas, making it chemically unreactive under normal conditions.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Xe
  • Atomic Number: 54
  • Atomic Mass: 131.29 amu
  • Density: 5.897 g/L
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas
  • Melting Point: -111.8°C
  • Boiling Point: -108.1°C

Importance in the Universe

Xenon is a rare gas in the Earth's atmosphere and is used in a variety of applications, including lighting and medical imaging.

Key Applications

Xenon is used in:

  • Lighting: Xenon is used in high-intensity discharge lamps, such as in car headlights and movie projectors.
  • Medical Imaging: Xenon is used as a contrast agent in imaging techniques like CT scans.
  • Space Exploration: Xenon is used as a propellant in ion thrusters for spacecraft propulsion.

Did You Know?

Xenon is one of the noble gases, which means it does not readily form compounds with other elements.

Isotopes of Xenon

Xenon has several isotopes, with Xenon-129 and Xenon-131 being the most stable and commonly found isotopes.

Chemical Properties

While Xenon is largely inert, it can form compounds under special conditions, such as:

  • Xenon Hexafluoride (XeF₆): A highly reactive compound used in chemical research.
  • Xenon Difluoride (XeF₂): Used in oxidation reactions in the laboratory.
