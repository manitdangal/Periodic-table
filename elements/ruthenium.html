Ruthenium

Ruthenium, symbol Ru, is the 44th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 44. It is a rare metal known for its hardness and excellent catalytic properties.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ru
  • Atomic Number: 44
  • Atomic Mass: 101.07 amu
  • Density: 12.37 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 2334°C
  • Boiling Point: 4150°C

Importance in the Universe

Ruthenium is found in trace amounts in asteroids and meteorites and is essential for the synthesis of heavier elements in stars.

Key Applications

Ruthenium has several key industrial applications:

  • Catalysis: Ruthenium is used as a catalyst in hydrogenation reactions, particularly in the chemical industry.
  • Electrical Contacts: Ruthenium is used to make electrical contacts and resistors due to its excellent conductivity.
  • Jewelry: Ruthenium is often used as a coating for jewelry to enhance its durability and finish.

Did You Know?

Ruthenium is named after the Latin word "Ruthenia," which refers to the region of Russia where the metal was first discovered.

Isotopes of Ruthenium

Ruthenium has seven isotopes, with Ruthenium-102 being the most stable and abundant.

Chemical Properties

Ruthenium is highly reactive and forms compounds like:

  • Ruthenium Dioxide (RuO₂): A catalyst used in various industrial processes, including the production of synthetic fuels.
  • Ruthenium Chloride (RuCl₃): A compound used in organic synthesis and as a catalyst in fuel cell technology.
