Neptunium

Neptunium, symbol Np, is an actinide metal with atomic number 93. It is the first transuranium element, discovered in 1940, and is a radioactive material used in scientific research and nuclear technology.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Np
  • Atomic Number: 93
  • Atomic Mass: 237.05
  • Density: 20.45 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 644 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,233 °C (estimated)

Importance in the Universe

Neptunium has little natural abundance and is primarily produced synthetically in nuclear reactors. It is important in the study of nuclear fission and is a key material for research into nuclear reactors and nuclear weaponry.

Key Applications

  • Scientific Research: Neptunium isotopes are used in neutron sources and as a fuel in some experimental nuclear reactors.
  • Military: Neptunium-239 is a precursor to plutonium-239, which is used in nuclear weapons and reactors.

Did You Know?

Neptunium was named after the planet Neptune, following the naming convention of elements discovered after uranium, which was named after the planet Uranus.

Isotopes of Neptunium

Neptunium has several isotopes, the most notable being Neptunium-239, which decays to form Plutonium-239. Neptunium-237 is the most stable isotope, with a half-life of 2.14 million years.

Chemical Properties

Neptunium is a highly radioactive metal that forms compounds in oxidation states ranging from +3 to +7. It is highly reactive, especially with halogens, and is used in nuclear fission processes.
