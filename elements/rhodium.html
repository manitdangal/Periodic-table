Rhodium

Rhodium, symbol Rh, is the 45th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 45. It is a rare, precious metal known for its remarkable resistance to corrosion and tarnish.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Rh
  • Atomic Number: 45
  • Atomic Mass: 102.91 amu
  • Density: 12.41 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1964°C
  • Boiling Point: 3690°C

Importance in the Universe

Rhodium is a rare metal found in small quantities in asteroids and meteorites, playing a role in star formation and element synthesis.

Key Applications

Rhodium is widely used in industrial applications, especially in:

  • Catalysis: Rhodium is a catalyst in automotive catalytic converters, helping reduce harmful emissions.
  • Jewelry: Rhodium plating is used to improve the appearance and durability of white gold and platinum jewelry.
  • Electronics: Rhodium is used in electrical contacts and connectors due to its excellent conductivity and corrosion resistance.

Did You Know?

Rhodium is one of the most expensive metals due to its rarity and high demand in catalytic converters.

Isotopes of Rhodium

Rhodium has a single stable isotope, Rhodium-103, which is commonly used in various applications.

Chemical Properties

Rhodium is chemically inert and forms compounds such as:

  • Rhodium Chloride (RhCl₃): Used as a catalyst in the production of acetic acid.
  • Rhodium Oxide (Rh₂O₃): Employed in industrial catalysis, especially for hydrogenation reactions.
