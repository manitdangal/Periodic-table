Aluminum

Aluminum, symbol Al, is the thirteenth element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 13. It is a silvery-white, soft, and non-magnetic metal that is widely used in industries ranging from transportation to construction.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Al
  • Atomic Number: 13
  • Atomic Mass: 26.982 amu
  • Density: 2.70 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 660.3°C
  • Boiling Point: 2470°C

Importance in the Universe

Aluminum is the most abundant metal in the Earth's crust, making up about 8% by weight. It plays a key role in the Earth's structure and is found in various minerals, most notably in bauxite ore, which is the primary source for its extraction.

Key Applications

Aluminum has a wide range of industrial and commercial uses:

  • Transportation: Aluminum is used extensively in the aerospace, automotive, and rail industries due to its light weight and strength-to-weight ratio.
  • Construction: Aluminum is a popular material in the construction of buildings, windows, doors, and roofing due to its corrosion resistance and durability.
  • Packaging: Aluminum is used in packaging materials such as cans, foils, and containers due to its non-toxic nature and ability to protect contents from air and moisture.
  • Electrical Wiring: Aluminum is used in electrical transmission lines and cables due to its good conductivity and lightweight properties.

Did You Know?

Aluminum is fully recyclable without losing its properties, making it one of the most sustainable materials in use today. Recycling aluminum saves up to 95% of the energy required to produce new aluminum from ore.

Chemical Properties

Aluminum has several key chemical characteristics:

  • Reactivity: Aluminum is highly reactive with oxygen, forming a thin, protective oxide layer that prevents further corrosion.
  • Reaction with Acids: Aluminum reacts with acids like hydrochloric acid (HCl) to release hydrogen gas and form aluminum chloride (AlCl₃).
  • Formation of Compounds: Aluminum forms a wide range of compounds, including aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃), aluminum sulfate (Al₂(SO₄)₃), and aluminum hydroxide (Al(OH)₃), which have various industrial applications.
