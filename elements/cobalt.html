Cobalt

Cobalt, symbol Co, is the 27th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 27. It is a transition metal that is commonly found in various minerals, often combined with other elements like arsenic and sulfur. Cobalt is widely used in the production of alloys and batteries.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Co
  • Atomic Number: 27
  • Atomic Mass: 58.933 amu
  • Density: 8.90 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1495°C
  • Boiling Point: 2927°C

Importance in the Universe

Cobalt is a rare element, essential for life in trace amounts, especially in vitamin B12. It also plays a crucial role in the formation of stars. In nature, cobalt is found in meteorites and various ores.

Key Applications

Cobalt has several important applications in industry and technology:

  • Alloys: Cobalt is used to produce high-strength alloys that can withstand high temperatures and corrosion, making it important for jet engines and turbines.
  • Batteries: Cobalt is used in lithium-ion batteries, which power devices like smartphones and electric vehicles.
  • Magnets: Cobalt-based magnets are used in electronics, including speakers and magnetic recording media.
  • Radioactive Isotope: Cobalt-60 is used in medical treatments, particularly in cancer therapy, as a radiation source.
