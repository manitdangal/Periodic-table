Dysprosium

Dysprosium, symbol Dy, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 66. It is a silvery-white metal that exhibits excellent magnetic properties and is used in a variety of advanced technological applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Dy
  • Atomic Number: 66
  • Atomic Mass: 162.50
  • Density: 8.540 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,412 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2,567 °C

Importance in the Universe

Dysprosium is highly valued for its unique magnetic and thermal properties. It is commonly used in magnets for wind turbines, electric vehicles, and in nuclear reactors due to its ability to absorb neutrons effectively.

Key Applications

Dysprosium is used in several important applications:

  • Magnets: Dysprosium is a critical component in neodymium-iron-boron magnets, enhancing their performance at high temperatures.
  • Nuclear Reactors: Dysprosium is used as a control material in nuclear reactors due to its high neutron absorption cross-section.
  • Lighting: Dysprosium compounds are used in lighting, including halide lamps that mimic natural sunlight.

Did You Know?

Dysprosium is one of the most magnetically susceptible elements, making it essential in the development of advanced magnetic systems.

Isotopes of Dysprosium

Dysprosium has several stable isotopes, the most abundant being Dysprosium-164. These isotopes are used in research and various industrial applications.

Chemical Properties

Dysprosium typically forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Dysprosium(III) oxide and Dysprosium(III) chloride. It reacts with oxygen and water at elevated temperatures, forming a protective oxide layer.
