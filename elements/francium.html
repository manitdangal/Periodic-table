Francium

Francium, symbol Fr, is an extremely rare and highly radioactive alkali metal with atomic number 87. It is the least stable of the alkali metals and is found in trace amounts in uranium and thorium ores.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Fr
  • Atomic Number: 87
  • Atomic Mass: 223
  • Density: 1.87 g/cm³ (estimated)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 27 °C (estimated)
  • Boiling Point: 677 °C (estimated)

Importance in the Universe

Francium is the least stable of the alkali metals and is extremely rare in nature. It is produced through the decay of heavier elements and has limited practical use due to its rarity and radioactivity.

Key Applications

  • Scientific Research: Francium is used primarily in scientific experiments, especially in the study of radioactive decay and nuclear reactions.
  • Potential for Medical Use: Research is ongoing to explore the potential use of francium in radiotherapy, though its scarcity and radioactivity limit its use.

Did You Know?

Francium is so rare that only a few grams of it exist on Earth at any given time.

Isotopes of Francium

Francium has several isotopes, with Francium-223 being the most stable, though it has a very short half-life of only 22 minutes.

Chemical Properties

As an alkali metal, francium is highly reactive and shares similar chemical properties with other alkali metals such as sodium and potassium. It reacts vigorously with water to produce hydrogen gas and heat.
