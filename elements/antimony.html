Tin

Tin, symbol Sn, is the 50th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 50. It is a silvery-white metal known for its corrosion resistance and is commonly used in alloys and coatings.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Sn
  • Atomic Number: 50
  • Atomic Mass: 118.71 amu
  • Density: 7.31 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 231.9°C
  • Boiling Point: 2602°C

Importance in the Universe

Tin is relatively abundant in the Earth's crust and has been used by humans for thousands of years. Its ability to resist corrosion makes it valuable in protecting metals from rusting.

Key Applications

Tin is primarily used in:

  • Alloys: Tin is a major component of bronze and pewter alloys.
  • Coatings: Tin is used to coat steel, especially in the production of tin cans for food preservation.
  • Solder: Tin is widely used in soldering metals, especially in electronics and plumbing.

Did You Know?

Tin was used by ancient civilizations to create bronze, an alloy that revolutionized tool and weapon-making.

Isotopes of Tin

Tin has ten stable isotopes, with Tin-120 and Tin-118 being the most abundant.

Chemical Properties

Tin forms compounds such as:

  • Tin Oxide (SnO₂): Used in the production of glass and ceramics.
  • Tin Chloride (SnCl₂): A compound used in chemical reactions and as a reducing agent.
