Europium

Europium, symbol Eu, is a chemical element with atomic number 63. Europium is one of the most reactive rare-earth elements and is known for its use in phosphorescent and fluorescent applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Eu
  • Atomic Number: 63
  • Atomic Mass: 151.98
  • Density: 5.264 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,426 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2,466 °C

Importance in the Universe

Europium is significant in both industrial and technological applications due to its ability to emit light when exposed to ultraviolet radiation. It is primarily used in fluorescent applications and in the production of phosphors for television screens and other display technologies.

Key Applications

Europium is used in a variety of applications:

  • Phosphors: Europium compounds are used in red phosphors for color television and computer monitors.
  • Fluorescent Lighting: Europium is used in some types of fluorescent lighting.
