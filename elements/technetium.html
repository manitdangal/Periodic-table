Technetium

Technetium, symbol Tc, is the 43rd element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 43. It is a radioactive element that was the first element to be artificially produced.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Tc
  • Atomic Number: 43
  • Atomic Mass: 98 amu
  • Density: 11.5 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 2157°C
  • Boiling Point: 4265°C

Importance in the Universe

Technetium is one of the rarest naturally occurring elements and is found in trace amounts in stars. It is essential in nuclear science due to its use in radiographic imaging.

Key Applications

Technetium has applications in various fields:

  • Medical Imaging: Technetium-99m is used as a tracer in nuclear medicine for imaging and diagnostic purposes.
  • Research: It is used in the study of nuclear reactions and in the creation of certain isotopes.

Did You Know?

Technetium was the first element to be artificially produced, synthesized by Italian scientists Carlo Perrier and Emilio Segrè in 1937.

Isotopes of Technetium

Technetium has no stable isotopes, but Technetium-99 is one of the most commonly used isotopes in medicine.

Chemical Properties

Technetium is chemically similar to manganese and forms compounds such as:

  • Technetium Chloride (TcCl₄): Used in research and industrial applications.
  • Technetium Oxide (TcO₂): A compound used in the production of catalysts and superconducting materials.
