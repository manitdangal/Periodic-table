Lanthanum

Lanthanum, symbol La, is the 57th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 57. It is a soft, silvery-white, malleable metal often classified as one of the rare earth elements.

General Properties

  • Symbol: La
  • Atomic Number: 57
  • Atomic Mass: 138.91 amu
  • Density: 6.15 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 920°C
  • Boiling Point: 3464°C

Importance in the Universe

Lanthanum is used in the production of strong magnets, catalysts for petroleum refining, and in lighting applications.

Key Applications

Lanthanum is used in:

  • Lighting: Lanthanum is used in high-intensity carbon arc lamps for motion pictures.
  • Alloys: Lanthanum is used to improve the properties of steel and other metals.
  • Battery Technology: Lanthanum is used in nickel-metal hydride batteries.

Did You Know?

Lanthanum is one of the rare earth elements and has important uses in modern technology, particularly in electronics and clean energy systems.

Isotopes of Lanthanum

The most stable isotope of Lanthanum is Lanthanum-138, which is used in many industrial applications.

Chemical Properties

Lanthanum forms compounds such as:

  • Lanthanum Oxide (La₂O₃): Used in catalysts and optical lenses.
  • Lanthanum Carbonate (La₂(CO₃)₃): Used in medical applications to treat hyperphosphatemia in kidney disease patients.
