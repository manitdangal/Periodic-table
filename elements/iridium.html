Iridium

Iridium, symbol Ir, is a transition metal with atomic number 77. It is one of the densest elements and is known for its incredible corrosion resistance, even at high temperatures. Its bright silvery appearance and rarity make it a sought-after metal for specialized applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ir
  • Atomic Number: 77
  • Atomic Mass: 192.22
  • Density: 22.56 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 2,446 °C
  • Boiling Point: 4,428 °C

Importance in the Universe

Iridium is a rare element found in meteorites and is used as a marker for extraterrestrial impacts. Its exceptional density and durability make it ideal for high-pressure and high-temperature environments.

Key Applications

  • Electronics: Used in spark plugs and electrical contacts for its high conductivity.
  • Scientific Instruments: Iridium crucibles are used in laboratories for high-temperature experiments.
  • Jewelry: Iridium alloys are used in fine jewelry and luxury watches for their durability.

Did You Know?

Iridium was named after the Greek goddess Iris, inspired by the colorful compounds it forms.

Isotopes of Iridium

Iridium has two stable isotopes, Iridium-191 and Iridium-193, which are used in industrial radiography and medical applications.

Chemical Properties

Iridium is highly inert, resistant to oxidation, and forms compounds like Iridium(IV) oxide. It is primarily used in alloy form to enhance hardness and corrosion resistance.
