Polonium

Polonium, symbol Po, is a highly radioactive element with atomic number 84. It is a rare element that is used in a limited number of applications due to its extreme toxicity and radioactivity.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Po
  • Atomic Number: 84
  • Atomic Mass: 209
  • Density: 9.32 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 254 °C
  • Boiling Point: 962 °C

Importance in the Universe

Polonium is a product of the decay of uranium and thorium and is found in trace amounts in the Earth's crust. Its radioactivity and rarity make it a subject of interest in scientific research and specialized applications.

Key Applications

  • Alpha Particle Source: Polonium is used as an alpha particle source in devices like ionization chambers and some nuclear applications.
  • Heat Source: Polonium-210 is used in space probes as a heat source due to its high energy release from alpha decay.
  • Poison: Polonium has been used in assassination attempts due to its radioactive properties and toxicity.

Did You Know?

Polonium gained notoriety when it was used in the assassination of former Russian FSB officer Alexander Litvinenko in 2006.

Isotopes of Polonium

Polonium has several isotopes, with Polonium-210 being the most well-known. This isotope has a half-life of 138.4 days and emits highly toxic alpha particles.

Chemical Properties

Polonium is highly reactive and can form compounds like Polonium(IV) chloride. Due to its radioactivity, it poses serious health risks, and its handling is strictly regulated in scientific settings.
