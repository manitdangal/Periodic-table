Rhenium

Rhenium, symbol Re, is a transition metal with atomic number 75. It is one of the densest and rarest elements in the Earth's crust, known for its high melting point and excellent resistance to wear.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Re
  • Atomic Number: 75
  • Atomic Mass: 186.21
  • Density: 21.02 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 3,186 °C
  • Boiling Point: 5,596 °C

Importance in the Universe

Rhenium is used in high-performance materials, particularly in aerospace and industrial settings where extreme temperatures and wear resistance are required.

Key Applications

Rhenium is utilized in the following fields:

  • Jet Engines: Alloys containing rhenium improve the durability and efficiency of turbine blades.
  • Catalysis: Rhenium is a critical component in catalysts for petrochemical refining.
  • Thermocouples: Used for high-temperature measurement devices.

Did You Know?

Rhenium was discovered in 1925, making it one of the last naturally occurring elements to be identified.

Isotopes of Rhenium

Rhenium has two naturally occurring isotopes: Rhenium-185 and Rhenium-187, with the latter being slightly radioactive and used in scientific research.

Chemical Properties

Rhenium forms compounds like Rhenium(VII) oxide and Rhenium(VII) chloride. It is resistant to corrosion and oxidation, even at high temperatures.
