Terbium

Terbium, symbol Tb, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 65. It is a silvery-green metal that is used in a variety of high-tech applications, including in phosphors and solid-state devices.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Tb
  • Atomic Number: 65
  • Atomic Mass: 158.93
  • Density: 8.229 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,356 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,230 °C

Importance in the Universe

Terbium is used in a range of technological applications, especially in phosphors that emit green light in various devices. It is found in trace amounts in the Earth's crust and is commonly used in the production of high-efficiency light bulbs and in certain types of magnets.

Key Applications

Terbium is used in several important applications:

  • Phosphors: Terbium compounds are used in phosphors for energy-efficient lighting, such as in fluorescent lamps.
  • Magnetostrictive Materials: Terbium is used in alloys for magnetostrictive applications, which are used in sonar and actuators.
  • Solid-State Devices: Terbium is used in various high-tech solid-state devices, such as in lasers and in certain types of sensors.

Did You Know?

Terbium is one of the key elements used in the production of green phosphors for color television and other display technologies.

Isotopes of Terbium

Terbium has several isotopes, the most stable being Terbium-159. It also has a radioactive isotope, Terbium-158, which is used in research applications.

Chemical Properties

Terbium typically forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Terbium(III) oxide and Terbium(III) chloride. It is highly reactive with oxygen and water at high temperatures.
