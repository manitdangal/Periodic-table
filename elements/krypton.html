Krypton

Krypton, symbol Kr, is the 36th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 36. It is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless noble gas that is chemically inert.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Kr
  • Atomic Number: 36
  • Atomic Mass: 83.798 amu
  • Density: 3.749 g/L (at 0°C and 1 atm)
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas
  • Melting Point: -157.36°C
  • Boiling Point: -153.22°C

Importance in the Universe

Krypton is a trace element found in Earth's atmosphere and is produced in stars through nuclear fusion. It is used in lighting and other high-tech applications.

Key Applications

Krypton is used in specialized applications:

  • Lighting: Krypton is used in light bulbs and in the production of high-efficiency lighting systems.
  • Lasers: Krypton is used in lasers for medical and industrial applications.
  • Insulation: Krypton is used in energy-efficient windows due to its ability to serve as an inert insulating gas.

Did You Know?

Krypton was discovered in 1898 by Sir William Ramsay and Morris Travers. Its name is derived from the Greek word "kryptos," meaning "hidden."

Isotopes of Krypton

Krypton has several isotopes, with Krypton-86 being the most abundant. These isotopes are used in scientific research and applications such as dating.

Chemical Properties

Krypton is chemically inert and does not readily form compounds. However, it can form some compounds under extreme conditions:

  • Krypton Fluoride (KrF₂): A reactive compound formed with fluorine.
