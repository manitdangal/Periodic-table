Strontium

Strontium, symbol Sr, is the 38th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 38. It is an alkaline earth metal that is highly reactive and burns with a bright red flame.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Sr
  • Atomic Number: 38
  • Atomic Mass: 87.62 amu
  • Density: 2.64 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 777°C
  • Boiling Point: 1382°C

Importance in the Universe

Strontium is found in small amounts in Earth's crust and plays an important role in the formation of stars. It also has applications in the study of nuclear energy.

Key Applications

Strontium is used in several applications:

  • Flame Colors: Strontium salts are used to produce red colors in fireworks and road flares.
  • Magnetism: Strontium ferrite is used in magnets and is a key component in the manufacture of magnetic materials.
  • Medical Imaging: Strontium-89 is used in cancer treatment, particularly for pain management in bone cancer.

Did You Know?

Strontium was discovered in 1790 by William Cruickshank in the mineral strontianite, which was named after the village of Strontian in Scotland.

Isotopes of Strontium

Strontium has several isotopes, with Strontium-90 being the most significant due to its use in nuclear reactors and medical treatments.

Chemical Properties

Strontium is highly reactive, particularly with water, and forms compounds such as:

  • Strontium Nitrate (Sr(NO₃)₂): Used in fireworks and in the production of red flares.
  • Strontium Carbonate (SrCO₃): Used in the production of ceramic materials and as a colorant in fireworks.
