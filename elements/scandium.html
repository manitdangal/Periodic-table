Scandium

Scandium, symbol Sc, is the 21st element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 21. It is a silvery-white metal that is soft and has a relatively low melting point. Scandium is found in trace amounts in various minerals and is used in a variety of high-performance applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Sc
  • Atomic Number: 21
  • Atomic Mass: 44.956 amu
  • Density: 2.985 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1541°C
  • Boiling Point: 2836°C

Importance in the Universe

Scandium is not abundant in the Earth’s crust but is important for the formation of certain types of stars and galaxies. It is often found in rare minerals, and its presence in certain meteorites and space dust suggests its role in stellar processes.

Key Applications

Scandium has a variety of important applications in modern industries:

  • Aerospace Industry: Scandium is used in the production of aluminum alloys, which are important for aerospace components due to their strength and light weight.
  • Solid-State Lasers: Scandium is used in the production of solid-state lasers, which are employed in various military, medical, and industrial applications.
  • Sports Equipment: It is used in high-performance sports equipment such as bicycles, baseball bats, and golf clubs.
  • Fuel Cells: Scandium is used in some types of fuel cells, enhancing their efficiency and longevity.

Did You Know?

Scandium was the first element to be discovered using the process of spectrum analysis, which involves studying the light emitted by a substance when heated. It was discovered in 1879 by Lars Fredrik Nilson in Sweden.

Isotopes of Scandium

Scandium has only one stable isotope, Scandium-45 (Sc-45), which accounts for all naturally occurring scandium. Scandium-45 is used in a variety of scientific applications, particularly in research on atomic structure and materials science.

Chemical Properties

Scandium is chemically similar to the other transition metals and reacts with water and acids to form scandium compounds:

  • Scandium Chloride (ScCl₃): A compound used in the production of specialty ceramics and advanced materials.
  • Scandium Oxide (Sc₂O₃): Used in the manufacturing of high-performance semiconductors and as a material in lighting applications.
  • Scandium Sulfate (Sc₂(SO₄)₃): Employed in research and the production of high-purity materials.
