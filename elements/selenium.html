Selenium

Selenium, symbol Se, is the 34th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 34. It is a non-metal element that occurs in various forms, including a gray metallic solid and a red powder.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Se
  • Atomic Number: 34
  • Atomic Mass: 78.971 amu
  • Density: 4.81 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 221°C
  • Boiling Point: 685°C

Importance in the Universe

Selenium is a trace element found in many stars, and it plays a crucial role in Earth's biogeochemical cycles. It is an essential component of some enzymes and proteins in living organisms.

Key Applications

Selenium is used in several industries and applications:

  • Solar Cells: Selenium is used in photovoltaic cells to convert sunlight into electricity.
  • Glass Manufacturing: Selenium is added to glass to give it a red or pink color and to reduce the glare in mirrors.
  • Electronics: Selenium is used in rectifiers and photocells.
  • Nutrition: Selenium is an essential micronutrient required for the function of certain enzymes in the body.

Did You Know?

Selenium was discovered in 1817 by Swedish chemist Jöns Jacob Berzelius. It was named after the Moon (Greek: "selene") because of its similarity to tellurium, which is named after the Earth.

Isotopes of Selenium

Selenium has several isotopes, with Selenium-80 being the most stable. Selenium-79, with a long half-life, is important for nuclear science research.

Chemical Properties

Selenium is chemically similar to sulfur, forming compounds such as:

  • Selenium Dioxide (SeO₂): A colorless solid used in the production of glass and pigments.
  • Selenium Sulfide (SeS₂): Used in dandruff treatments and as a fungicide.
  • Sodium Selenate (Na₂SeO₄): Used in chemical synthesis and in agricultural applications.
