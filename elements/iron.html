Iron

Iron, symbol Fe, is the 26th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 26. It is a metal with magnetic properties and is essential for life. Iron is commonly found in the Earth's crust and is a key component in the production of steel.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Fe
  • Atomic Number: 26
  • Atomic Mass: 55.845 amu
  • Density: 7.874 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1538°C
  • Boiling Point: 2862°C

Importance in the Universe

Iron is the most abundant element by mass on Earth and is the most abundant heavy element formed in stars. It is essential for life and plays a critical role in the formation of red blood cells in humans and other organisms.

Key Applications

Iron has numerous applications across various industries:

  • Steel Production: Iron is a key component in making steel, a material essential for construction, transportation, and machinery.
  • Magnetic Materials: Iron is used in the manufacture of magnets and electronic devices due to its ferromagnetic properties.
  • Biological Systems: Iron is essential in hemoglobin, the protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body.
  • Industrial Uses: Iron is also used in the production of cast iron, an alloy used in engine blocks and pipes.

Did You Know?

Iron is one of the most important elements for sustaining life. It is found in hemoglobin, which transports oxygen in the blood, and is also crucial for energy production in cells.

Isotopes of Iron

Iron has several isotopes, with Iron-56 being the most stable and abundant in nature. Iron-57 is commonly used in scientific research, especially in Mössbauer spectroscopy.

Chemical Properties

Iron is highly reactive, especially at high temperatures, and forms a wide variety of compounds:

  • Iron Oxide (Fe₂O₃): Known as rust, it forms when iron reacts with oxygen in the presence of water or moisture.
  • Iron Sulfide (FeS): Formed when iron reacts with sulfur, it is used in the production of iron-based chemicals.
  • Iron Chloride (FeCl₃): Used as a catalyst in various chemical processes.
