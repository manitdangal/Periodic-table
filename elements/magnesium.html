Magnesium

Magnesium, symbol Mg, is the twelfth element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 12. It is a shiny gray solid and is an alkaline earth metal. Magnesium is essential for life, particularly for the proper functioning of cells, enzymes, and muscles.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Mg
  • Atomic Number: 12
  • Atomic Mass: 24.305 amu
  • Density: 1.738 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 650°C
  • Boiling Point: 1090°C

Importance in the Universe

Magnesium is the eighth most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is also found in significant amounts in seawater and is essential for all living organisms. Magnesium plays a central role in biological functions such as ATP synthesis and enzyme activation.

Key Applications

Magnesium is used in a variety of industries and applications:

  • Aerospace and Automotive: Magnesium alloys are used in aircraft and automobile manufacturing due to their light weight and strength.
  • Electronics: Magnesium is used in the production of mobile phone and laptop casings.
  • Medicine: Magnesium compounds are used in antacids, laxatives, and supplements to treat magnesium deficiency.
  • Construction: Magnesium is used in the production of lightweight concrete and other building materials.

Did You Know?

Magnesium is a key component in chlorophyll, the molecule that plants use for photosynthesis. Without magnesium, plants would not be able to produce energy from sunlight.

Chemical Properties

Magnesium is a highly reactive metal and has the following chemical characteristics:

  • Reaction with Water: Magnesium reacts slowly with cold water but rapidly with hot water, producing hydrogen gas and magnesium hydroxide.
  • Burning in Air: Magnesium burns with a bright white light, forming magnesium oxide (MgO), which is an important material in refractory applications.
  • Formation of Compounds: Magnesium forms a variety of compounds, including magnesium sulfate (MgSO₄), magnesium carbonate (MgCO₃), and magnesium chloride (MgCl₂), all of which are used in different industrial processes.
