Boron

Boron, symbol B, is the fifth element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 5. It is a metalloid, exhibiting properties of both metals and non-metals. Boron is a hard, brittle element that plays a vital role in various chemical and industrial applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: B
  • Atomic Number: 5
  • Atomic Mass: 10.81 amu
  • Density: 2.34 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 2075°C
  • Boiling Point: 4000°C

Applications

Boron is used in a wide range of fields due to its chemical versatility and physical properties:

  • Glass Manufacturing: Boron compounds, such as borax, are used in producing heat-resistant borosilicate glass.
  • Agriculture: Boron is an essential micronutrient for plants, promoting healthy growth and development.
  • Ceramics: Boron is used in the production of high-strength ceramics and enamels.
  • Energy: Boron-based fuels are being researched for potential use in advanced energy applications.

Did You Know?

Boron is not found free in nature but exists in compounds like borates. The Mojave Desert in California is one of the world’s largest sources of borax.

Chemical Properties

Boron exhibits unique chemical properties and forms a variety of compounds:

  • Boron Trioxide (B₂O₃): A key component in the production of glass and ceramics.
  • Boron Hydrides: Used in rocket fuel and as reagents in organic synthesis.
  • Nonreactive Nature: Boron resists attack by most acids and alkalis, making it valuable in industrial applications.
