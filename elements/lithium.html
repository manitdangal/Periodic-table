Lithium

Lithium, symbol Li, is the third element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 3. It is a soft, silvery-white alkali metal, known for being the lightest metal and solid element at standard conditions.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Li
  • Atomic Number: 3
  • Atomic Mass: 6.94 amu
  • Density: 0.534 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 180.54°C
  • Boiling Point: 1342°C

Importance and Uses

Lithium is a highly reactive and versatile element with numerous applications:

  • Batteries: Lithium-ion batteries are widely used in electronics and electric vehicles.
  • Glass and Ceramics: Lithium compounds improve heat resistance and durability.
  • Pharmaceuticals: Lithium salts are used in treating bipolar disorder.

Did You Know?

Lithium is so light that it can float on water, but it reacts explosively with water to produce hydrogen gas and heat.

Chemical Properties

Lithium is highly reactive and forms compounds with many elements:

  • Lithium Hydroxide (LiOH): Formed when lithium reacts with water.
  • Lithium Chloride (LiCl): A salt used in air conditioning and dehumidifiers.
