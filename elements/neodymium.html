Neodymium

Neodymium, symbol Nd, is a chemical element with atomic number 60. It is a bright, silvery metal and is one of the rare-earth elements. Neodymium is highly magnetic and is most well-known for its use in strong permanent magnets.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Nd
  • Atomic Number: 60
  • Atomic Mass: 144.242
  • Density: 7.01 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,024 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,130 °C

Importance in the Universe

Neodymium is one of the most abundant of the rare earth elements and plays a critical role in modern technology due to its magnetic properties. It is essential in the manufacturing of powerful magnets used in various devices such as motors, headphones, and MRI machines.

Key Applications

Neodymium is used in various applications, including:

  • Magnets: Neodymium magnets are widely used in motors, microphones, and other high-tech applications.
  • Alloys: Neodymium is added to iron alloys to increase strength and resist corrosion.
  • Glass and Lasers: Neodymium is used in optical glass and in neodymium-doped laser applications.

Did You Know?

Neodymium magnets are among the strongest permanent magnets known and are used in applications ranging from computer hard drives to electric vehicle motors.

Isotopes of Neodymium

The most stable isotopes of Neodymium are Neodymium-142 and Neodymium-144. Neodymium-143, which is stable, is used in radiometric dating.

Chemical Properties

Neodymium typically forms a +3 oxidation state. It reacts slowly with oxygen and moisture in the air, and its compounds are known to be very reactive, especially when heated.
