Hafnium

Hafnium, symbol Hf, is a transition metal with atomic number 72. It is a lustrous, silvery metal known for its exceptional resistance to corrosion and high melting point, making it vital in nuclear technology and high-temperature applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Hf
  • Atomic Number: 72
  • Atomic Mass: 178.49
  • Density: 13.31 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 2,233 °C
  • Boiling Point: 4,603 °C

Importance in the Universe

Hafnium plays a critical role in nuclear reactors and electronics. Its exceptional ability to absorb neutrons makes it indispensable in controlling nuclear reactions. Additionally, hafnium is used in superalloys and microprocessors for its stability under extreme conditions.

Key Applications

Hafnium is used in several advanced applications:

  • Nuclear Control Rods: Due to its high neutron absorption, hafnium is used in control rods to regulate nuclear reactions.
  • High-Temperature Alloys: Hafnium is used in superalloys for jet engines and space exploration materials.
  • Microelectronics: Hafnium oxide is a key material in modern microprocessors and transistors.

Did You Know?

Hafnium was discovered in 1923 and named after "Hafnia," the Latin name for Copenhagen.

Isotopes of Hafnium

Hafnium has several stable isotopes, with Hafnium-178 being notable for its potential use in gamma-ray lasers. Its isotopes are also studied for nuclear and scientific applications.

Chemical Properties

Hafnium is highly resistant to corrosion due to a protective oxide layer. It forms compounds in the +4 oxidation state, such as Hafnium(IV) oxide and Hafnium(IV) chloride, which are vital in industrial processes.
