Rubidium

Rubidium, symbol Rb, is the 37th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 37. It is an alkali metal that is highly reactive and has a soft, silvery-white appearance.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Rb
  • Atomic Number: 37
  • Atomic Mass: 85.4678 amu
  • Density: 1.532 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 39.31°C
  • Boiling Point: 688°C

Importance in the Universe

Rubidium is found in trace amounts in Earth's crust and plays a role in the study of meteorites and planetary formation. It is also present in various cosmic phenomena.

Key Applications

Rubidium is used in a number of scientific and technological applications:

  • Atomic Clocks: Rubidium is used in highly accurate atomic clocks.
  • Research: Rubidium is used in experiments involving laser cooling and Bose-Einstein condensates.
  • Medical Imaging: Rubidium isotopes are used in positron emission tomography (PET) scans.

Did You Know?

Rubidium was discovered in 1861 by German chemists Robert Bunsen and Gustav Kirchhoff through the analysis of the mineral lepidolite.

Isotopes of Rubidium

Rubidium has two stable isotopes: Rubidium-85 and Rubidium-87. The latter is important for dating rocks and understanding geological processes.

Chemical Properties

Rubidium is highly reactive and forms compounds such as:

  • Rubidium Hydroxide (RbOH): A strong base used in chemical synthesis.
  • Rubidium Chloride (RbCl): A compound used in atomic research and as a catalyst in certain reactions.
