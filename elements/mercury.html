Mercury

Mercury, symbol Hg, is a transition metal with atomic number 80. Known as quicksilver, mercury is the only metal that is liquid at room temperature. It has been used since ancient times, though its toxic properties are now well understood.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Hg
  • Atomic Number: 80
  • Atomic Mass: 200.59
  • Density: 13.53 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Liquid
  • Melting Point: -38.83 °C
  • Boiling Point: 356.73 °C

Importance in the Universe

Mercury is formed during supernovae and is present in trace amounts in the Earth's crust. Its liquid form and unique properties make it essential in thermometers, barometers, and various scientific instruments.

Key Applications

  • Thermometers and Barometers: Mercury is commonly used in thermometers due to its consistent expansion in response to temperature changes.
  • Electrical Switches: Mercury is used in electrical switches, particularly in high-precision instruments.
  • Gold Mining: Mercury is used in amalgamation for gold extraction from ores.

Did You Know?

Despite being toxic, mercury was used in ancient medicine and in various industrial processes for centuries.

Isotopes of Mercury

Mercury has seven stable isotopes, with Mercury-202 being the most abundant. Mercury-199 is also important in medical applications such as cancer therapy research.

Chemical Properties

Mercury is highly toxic and forms compounds such as mercury(II) chloride and mercury(II) sulfide. These compounds are often used in industrial processes but are also dangerous to human health and the environment.
