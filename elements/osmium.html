Osmium

Osmium, symbol Os, is a transition metal with atomic number 76. It is the densest naturally occurring element, with remarkable hardness and brittleness. Its bluish-gray color and high resistance to wear make it valuable in industrial and scientific applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Os
  • Atomic Number: 76
  • Atomic Mass: 190.23
  • Density: 22.59 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 3,033 °C
  • Boiling Point: 5,012 °C

Importance in the Universe

Osmium is a rare element found in the Earth's crust and is often associated with platinum-group metals. It is highly valued in specialized applications due to its unmatched density and resistance to abrasion.

Key Applications

Osmium is used in various advanced technologies and industrial applications, such as:

  • Electrical Contacts: Osmium alloys are used in applications requiring high durability and conductivity, such as in electrical switches.
  • Pen Tips: Its hardness makes it ideal for fountain pen nibs and styluses.
  • Catalysts: Osmium tetroxide is widely used in organic synthesis and electron microscopy staining for its reactive properties.
  • Scientific Tools: Osmium is utilized in high-precision instruments due to its exceptional wear resistance.

Did You Know?

Osmium gets its name from the Greek word "osme," meaning smell, because its oxide emits a sharp, distinct odor.

Isotopes of Osmium

Osmium has seven naturally occurring isotopes, including Osmium-192, which is the most abundant. These isotopes are used in age-dating methods such as rhenium-osmium dating to determine the age of rocks and meteorites.

Chemical Properties

Osmium is resistant to acids but reacts with oxygen to form Osmium(VIII) oxide, a volatile and toxic compound. It is often found in alloy form, where its properties enhance the durability and strength of materials.
