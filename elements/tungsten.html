Tungsten

Tungsten, symbol W, is a transition metal with atomic number 74. It is renowned for its exceptional hardness, high melting point, and resistance to wear, making it invaluable in industrial and high-temperature applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: W
  • Atomic Number: 74
  • Atomic Mass: 183.84
  • Density: 19.25 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 3,422 °C
  • Boiling Point: 5,555 °C

Importance in the Universe

Tungsten is critical in industries that demand materials with exceptional strength and stability under extreme conditions. Its unique properties make it essential in aerospace, defense, and energy applications.

Key Applications

Tungsten is widely used in the following areas:

  • Light Bulb Filaments: Tungsten is used in incandescent bulbs for its ability to withstand high temperatures.
  • Cutting and Drilling Tools: Tungsten carbide is a key material in the manufacture of tools for its extreme hardness.
  • High-Temperature Components: Used in rocket engines and other applications requiring heat resistance.

Did You Know?

Tungsten has the highest melting point of all metals and is also one of the densest naturally occurring elements.

Isotopes of Tungsten

Tungsten has five stable isotopes, with Tungsten-182 being the most abundant. Its isotopes are valuable for research in geology and nuclear science.

Chemical Properties

Tungsten is chemically inert and highly resistant to attack by acids and bases. It forms compounds such as tungsten oxide and tungsten carbide, which are widely used in industrial applications.
