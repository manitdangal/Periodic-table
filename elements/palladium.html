Palladium

Palladium, symbol Pd, is the 46th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 46. It is a precious, silvery-white metal used extensively in industrial applications, especially in catalytic converters and hydrogen storage.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Pd
  • Atomic Number: 46
  • Atomic Mass: 106.42 amu
  • Density: 12.02 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1554.9°C
  • Boiling Point: 2963°C

Importance in the Universe

Palladium is relatively rare in the Earth's crust but is essential in processes such as hydrogen storage and purification. It is one of the noble metals, which means it is resistant to corrosion and oxidation.

Key Applications

Palladium is widely used in a variety of industrial applications:

  • Catalysis: Palladium is widely used in catalytic converters to reduce harmful emissions from vehicles.
  • Hydrogen Storage: Palladium absorbs large amounts of hydrogen and is used in hydrogen storage systems.
  • Electronics: Palladium is used in the production of electrical contacts and connectors due to its excellent conductivity.
  • Jewelry: Palladium is used in jewelry, often as an alloy with gold to produce white gold.

Did You Know?

Palladium was discovered in 1803 by William Hyde Wollaston, and it was named after the asteroid Pallas, which was discovered around the same time.

Isotopes of Palladium

Palladium has several isotopes, with Palladium-105 being the most stable isotope used in scientific applications.

Chemical Properties

Palladium is chemically similar to platinum and forms compounds such as:

  • Palladium Chloride (PdCl₂): Used in the production of hydrogenation catalysts.
  • Palladium Acetate (Pd(C₂H₃O₂)₂): Used as a catalyst in various organic reactions.
