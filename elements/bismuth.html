Bismuth

Bismuth, symbol Bi, is a post-transition metal with atomic number 83. It is a brittle, heavy metal that has a pinkish hue and is known for being one of the least toxic heavy metals.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Bi
  • Atomic Number: 83
  • Atomic Mass: 208.98
  • Density: 9.78 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 271.4 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1,564 °C

Importance in the Universe

Bismuth is present in trace amounts in the Earth's crust and is formed in stars. Its low toxicity and unique physical properties make it useful in various applications, including cosmetics, medicine, and industrial processes.

Key Applications

  • Cosmetics: Bismuth oxychloride is commonly used in cosmetics and skincare products due to its pearlescent properties.
  • Medicine: Bismuth compounds, such as Bismuth subsalicylate, are used in over-the-counter medications for digestive issues, including Pepto-Bismol.
  • Alloys: Bismuth is used in low-melting-point alloys for fire detection and extinguishing systems.

Did You Know?

Bismuth has the lowest thermal conductivity of any metal, making it useful in specialized applications that require heat insulation.

Isotopes of Bismuth

Bismuth has one stable isotope, Bismuth-209, which has an extremely long half-life and is used in scientific experiments. It was once thought to be the heaviest stable isotope, but it has since been shown to decay very slowly.

Chemical Properties

Bismuth is relatively unreactive compared to other metals. It resists oxidation and does not tarnish easily. It forms compounds such as Bismuth(III) oxide and Bismuth(III) chloride, which are used in various industrial and pharmaceutical applications.
