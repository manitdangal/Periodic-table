Cesium

Cesium, symbol Cs, is the 55th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 55. It is a soft, silvery-gold alkali metal, highly reactive and used in various applications, including atomic clocks.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Cs
  • Atomic Number: 55
  • Atomic Mass: 132.91 amu
  • Density: 1.93 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 28.44°C
  • Boiling Point: 671°C

Importance in the Universe

Cesium is one of the most reactive alkali metals and has many applications in scientific research, especially in the field of timekeeping and GPS systems.

Key Applications

Cesium is used in:

  • Atomic Clocks: Cesium is used to create highly accurate atomic clocks, crucial for GPS and telecommunications.
  • Oil Drilling: Cesium formate is used in drilling fluids for deep well drilling.
  • Medical Imaging: Cesium isotopes are used in cancer treatments.
