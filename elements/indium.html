Indium

Indium, symbol In, is the 49th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 49. It is a soft, malleable metal with a silvery-white appearance and is primarily used in electronics and as a coating material.

General Properties

  • Symbol: In
  • Atomic Number: 49
  • Atomic Mass: 114.82 amu
  • Density: 7.31 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 156.6°C
  • Boiling Point: 2072°C

Importance in the Universe

Indium is quite rare in the Earth's crust but plays a significant role in modern technologies due to its unique properties, especially in the semiconductor industry.

Key Applications

Indium is used in several high-tech applications:

  • Electronics: Indium is used in the production of touchscreens, flat-panel displays, and semiconductors.
  • Alloys: Indium is alloyed with other metals to improve their properties, such as in lead-free solders used in electronics.
  • Coatings: Indium is used to coat bearings and other machinery parts due to its ability to reduce friction.

Did You Know?

Indium is often used as a replacement for lead in solders due to its low melting point and non-toxic properties.

Isotopes of Indium

Indium has two stable isotopes: Indium-113 and Indium-115, with Indium-115 being the more abundant isotope.

Chemical Properties

Indium forms compounds such as:

  • Indium Oxide (In₂O₃): Used in the production of transparent conductive coatings.
  • Indium Trichloride (InCl₃): Used in the manufacturing of semiconductors.
