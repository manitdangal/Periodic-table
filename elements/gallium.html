Gallium

Gallium, symbol Ga, is the 31st element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 31. It is a soft metal with a melting point just above room temperature, making it a unique and valuable material in electronics and photonics.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ga
  • Atomic Number: 31
  • Atomic Mass: 69.723 amu
  • Density: 5.91 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 29.76°C
  • Boiling Point: 2204°C

Importance in the Universe

Gallium is found in trace amounts in stars and in Earth's crust. It plays a key role in various high-tech applications due to its semiconducting properties.

Key Applications

Gallium is used in a variety of high-tech applications:

  • Electronics: Gallium arsenide (GaAs) is used in semiconductors for high-frequency and high-power applications, such as in mobile phones and satellites.
  • Solar Cells: Gallium is used in the production of high-efficiency solar panels, particularly in multi-junction solar cells.
  • LEDs: Gallium nitride (GaN) is used to create blue and white LEDs, which are essential for modern lighting technology.

Did You Know?

Gallium is unique in that it can melt in your hand because its melting point is just slightly above room temperature. This property is one of the reasons why it has been used in semiconductors and electronic devices.

Isotopes of Gallium

Gallium has two stable isotopes: Gallium-69 and Gallium-71, with Gallium-69 being the most abundant. These isotopes are used in various scientific research applications.

Chemical Properties

Gallium reacts with oxygen and moisture, forming a protective oxide layer on its surface. It also reacts with acids and bases, forming salts like:

  • Gallium Nitride (GaN): Used in high-power electronics and LEDs.
  • Gallium Arsenide (GaAs): A compound used in semiconductors and optoelectronics.
