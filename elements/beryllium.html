Beryllium

Beryllium, symbol Be, is the fourth element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 4. It is a rare, lightweight, and hard alkaline earth metal with a silvery-white appearance. Beryllium is known for its high stiffness, low density, and excellent thermal conductivity.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Be
  • Atomic Number: 4
  • Atomic Mass: 9.0122 amu
  • Density: 1.848 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1287°C
  • Boiling Point: 2469°C

Applications

Beryllium is a critical material in various industries due to its unique properties:

  • Aerospace: Used in aircraft and spacecraft components due to its high strength-to-weight ratio.
  • Nuclear Reactors: Beryllium is employed as a moderator and reflector of neutrons.
  • Electronics: Used in manufacturing springs, connectors, and other electrical components.
  • Alloys: Beryllium-copper alloys are used in tools and machinery requiring non-sparking properties.

Did You Know?

Beryllium is toxic if inhaled as dust or fumes, and prolonged exposure can lead to a chronic disease called berylliosis.

Chemical Properties

Beryllium is chemically resistant to oxidation and has unique reactivity:

  • Beryllium Oxide (BeO): A compound with excellent thermal conductivity, used in electronics.
  • Beryllium Chloride (BeCl₂): Used in refining beryllium from ores.
  • Nonmagnetic: Beryllium does not retain magnetism, making it useful in specific industries.
