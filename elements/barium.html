Barium

Barium, symbol Ba, is the 56th element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 56. It is a soft, silvery alkaline earth metal that is highly reactive, especially with water.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ba
  • Atomic Number: 56
  • Atomic Mass: 137.33 amu
  • Density: 3.62 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 727°C
  • Boiling Point: 1640°C

Importance in the Universe

Barium is used in a variety of industries, from electronics to medical imaging, and is found in small quantities in the Earth's crust.

Key Applications

Barium is used in:

  • Medical Imaging: Barium sulfate is used as a radiopaque contrast medium in X-ray imaging.
  • Alloys: Barium is added to alloys to improve their properties, such as in the production of aluminum alloys for aircraft.
  • Fireworks: Barium compounds are used in fireworks to produce green colors.

Did You Know?

Barium is toxic, and exposure to its compounds can cause health problems, but it is safely used in controlled environments like medical imaging.

Isotopes of Barium

The most stable isotope of Barium is Barium-138, which is non-radioactive and abundant in nature.

Chemical Properties

Barium forms compounds such as:

  • Barium Nitrate (Ba(NO₃)₂): Used in the production of fireworks and other pyrotechnics.
  • Barium Carbonate (BaCO₃): Used in ceramics and as a rat poison.
