Thulium

Thulium, symbol Tm, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 69. It is a soft, silvery-gray metal that is the least abundant of the lanthanide series.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Tm
  • Atomic Number: 69
  • Atomic Mass: 168.93
  • Density: 9.32 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,545 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1,950 °C

Importance in the Universe

Thulium is used in specialized equipment, such as portable X-ray devices, due to its ability to emit radiation when activated. It is one of the rarest elements in the Earth's crust.

Key Applications

Thulium is used in several important applications:

  • X-Ray Devices: Thulium is used in compact, portable X-ray systems.
  • Lasers: Thulium-based lasers are used in surgical and medical procedures.
  • Research: Thulium isotopes are used in nuclear research and radiation sources.

Did You Know?

Thulium is named after "Thule," an ancient name for Scandinavia.

Isotopes of Thulium

Thulium has only one stable isotope, Thulium-169, which is used in radiation devices and medical research.

Chemical Properties

Thulium forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Thulium(III) oxide. It reacts with oxygen, forming a stable oxide layer.
