Holmium

Holmium, symbol Ho, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 67. It is a silvery-white metal known for its extraordinary magnetic properties.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Ho
  • Atomic Number: 67
  • Atomic Mass: 164.93
  • Density: 8.79 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,474 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2,700 °C

Importance in the Universe

Holmium is highly magnetic, making it valuable in creating high-strength magnets and in magnetic field studies. It is rare in the Earth's crust and primarily found in minerals like monazite and bastnäsite.

Key Applications

Holmium is used in several important applications:

  • Magnets: Holmium is used in powerful magnetic alloys and magnetic field generation.
  • Lasers: Holmium-based lasers are used in medical and dental procedures.
  • Nuclear Reactors: Holmium is used as a control material in nuclear reactors due to its neutron-absorbing properties.

Did You Know?

Holmium has the highest magnetic moment of any naturally occurring element!

Isotopes of Holmium

Holmium has one naturally occurring isotope, Holmium-165, which is stable and used in research and industrial applications.

Chemical Properties

Holmium typically forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Holmium(III) oxide. It reacts with oxygen and acids, forming various salts and compounds.
