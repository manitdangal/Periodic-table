Plutonium

Plutonium, symbol Pu, is a radioactive actinide element with atomic number 94. It is used primarily in nuclear weapons and nuclear reactors due to its ability to undergo fission.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Pu
  • Atomic Number: 94
  • Atomic Mass: 244.06
  • Density: 19.86 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 640 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,235 °C (estimated)

Importance in the Universe

Plutonium is one of the most important elements in nuclear energy, primarily used in nuclear reactors and atomic bombs. It is produced from uranium-238 in nuclear reactors and has applications in deep space missions.

Key Applications

  • Nuclear Energy: Plutonium-239 is used as fuel in nuclear reactors for electricity generation.
  • Weaponry: Plutonium-239 is one of the fissile materials used in the construction of nuclear weapons.
  • Space Exploration: Plutonium-238 is used in radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs) for spacecraft, providing power for missions beyond Earth's orbit.

Did You Know?

Plutonium was named after the planet Pluto, continuing the tradition of naming elements after planets (like Uranium after Uranus and Neptunium after Neptune).

Isotopes of Plutonium

Plutonium has several isotopes, the most significant being Plutonium-239, which is used in both nuclear reactors and nuclear weapons. Plutonium-238 is used in RTGs for space exploration.

Chemical Properties

Plutonium is highly radioactive and forms a variety of compounds, typically in the +3 and +4 oxidation states. It is highly toxic and must be handled with care in specialized facilities due to its radiation.
