Niobium

Niobium, symbol Nb, is the 41st element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 41. It is a transition metal known for its corrosion resistance and superconducting properties.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Nb
  • Atomic Number: 41
  • Atomic Mass: 92.906 amu
  • Density: 8.57 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 2477°C
  • Boiling Point: 4744°C

Importance in the Universe

Niobium is found in small quantities in the Earth's crust, and it is a key element in the formation of other complex materials in stars.

Key Applications

Niobium is used in various industries:

  • Aerospace: Niobium is used in the production of high-performance superalloys for jet engines and rockets.
  • Superconductivity: Niobium-titanium alloys are used in superconducting magnets for MRI machines and particle accelerators.
  • Steel Alloys: Niobium is added to steel to improve its strength, toughness, and resistance to corrosion.

Did You Know?

Niobium was discovered in 1801 by Charles Hatchett, and it was originally named columbium after Christopher Columbus.

Isotopes of Niobium

Niobium has only one naturally occurring isotope, Niobium-93, which is stable and does not undergo radioactive decay.

Chemical Properties

Niobium is highly resistant to corrosion and forms compounds like:

  • Niobium Oxide (Nb₂O₅): Used in electronics and as a catalyst in industrial processes.
  • Niobium Carbide (NbC): A material used in the production of high-strength alloys and cutting tools.
