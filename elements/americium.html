Americium

Americium, symbol Am, is an actinide element with atomic number 95. It is a synthetic radioactive element, first isolated in 1944, and is primarily used in smoke detectors and scientific research.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Am
  • Atomic Number: 95
  • Atomic Mass: 243.06
  • Density: 13.67 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,178 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2,300 °C (estimated)

Importance in the Universe

Americium is notable for its use in smoke detectors and its role in research on nuclear energy. It is an artificial element, produced through the irradiation of plutonium-239 in a nuclear reactor.

Key Applications

  • Smoke Detectors: Americium-241 is used as a source of ionizing radiation in ionization-type smoke detectors, helping to detect smoke in homes and buildings.
  • Scientific Research: Americium is used in research involving nuclear reactions, including studies on nuclear fuel cycles and radioactivity.
  • Industrial Gauging: Americium is used in devices for measuring thickness and density in manufacturing processes.

Did You Know?

Americium was named after the Americas, following the naming convention of elements named after continents (like Europium). It was discovered in the early 1940s during the development of the atomic bomb.

Isotopes of Americium

The most stable isotope, Americium-243, has a half-life of 7,370 years, while Americium-241 is the most commonly used isotope in commercial applications.

Chemical Properties

Americium is a highly radioactive metal that forms compounds in oxidation states ranging from +3 to +6. It is reactive with oxygen and reacts slowly with water and acids to form americium compounds.
