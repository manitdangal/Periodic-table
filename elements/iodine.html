Iodine

Iodine, symbol I, is the 53rd element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 53. It is a halogen, often found in its molecular form, and plays a crucial role in human health and medicine.

General Properties

  • Symbol: I
  • Atomic Number: 53
  • Atomic Mass: 126.90 amu
  • Density: 4.93 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 113.7°C
  • Boiling Point: 184.3°C

Importance in the Universe

Iodine is essential for the synthesis of thyroid hormones in the human body and is found in trace amounts in the Earth's crust.

Key Applications

Iodine is used in the following fields:

  • Medicine: Iodine is used as a disinfectant, and iodized salt is an important source of dietary iodine to prevent deficiency.
  • Photography: Iodine compounds are used in photographic film development.
  • Thyroid Treatment: Iodine-131 is used in the treatment of thyroid cancer.

Did You Know?

Iodine was historically used in the form of tinctures to treat wounds and as an antiseptic.

Isotopes of Iodine

Iodine has several isotopes, with Iodine-131 being used in medical treatments and Iodine-127 being the most stable isotope.

Chemical Properties

Iodine forms compounds such as:

  • Iodine Trichloride (ICl₃): A compound used in organic chemistry.
  • Potassium Iodide (KI): Used in medicine and as a source of iodine for nutrition.
