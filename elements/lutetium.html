Lutetium

Lutetium, symbol Lu, is a rare-earth metal and the final element in the lanthanide series, with atomic number 71. It is a hard, silvery-white metal often used in specialized industrial and medical applications.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Lu
  • Atomic Number: 71
  • Atomic Mass: 174.97
  • Density: 9.84 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,663 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3,402 °C

Importance in the Universe

Lutetium has unique uses in fields like radiopharmaceuticals and advanced technology. It is one of the densest and hardest lanthanides, making it valuable for specialized applications in medical imaging and catalysts.

Key Applications

Lutetium is used in several specialized applications:

  • Medical Imaging: Lutetium isotopes are used in PET scanners and radiotherapy.
  • Catalysts: Lutetium compounds are used as catalysts in petroleum refining.
  • Optics: Lutetium-doped crystals are used in lasers for high-precision cutting and communication technologies.

Did You Know?

Lutetium was discovered in 1907 and named after "Lutetia," the Latin name for Paris.

Isotopes of Lutetium

Lutetium has two naturally occurring isotopes: Lutetium-175, which is stable, and Lutetium-176, which is radioactive and used in geological dating and medical research.

Chemical Properties

Lutetium forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Lutetium(III) oxide. It is relatively stable in air but reacts with acids, forming salts.
