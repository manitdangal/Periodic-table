Erbium

Erbium, symbol Er, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 68. It is a silvery-white metal known for its role in optical and laser technologies.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Er
  • Atomic Number: 68
  • Atomic Mass: 167.26
  • Density: 9.07 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,494 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2,900 °C

Importance in the Universe

Erbium is widely used in optical communication systems as a key component of fiber-optic amplifiers. It is primarily extracted from minerals like monazite and xenotime.

Key Applications

Erbium is used in several important applications:

  • Optical Fibers: Erbium-doped fibers are crucial for amplifying signals in long-distance optical communication systems.
  • Lasers: Erbium is used in lasers for medical and dental applications, as well as in dermatology.
  • Alloys: Erbium is used in some specialized alloys to improve their properties.

Did You Know?

Erbium's pink salts are often used to give a rose-colored tint to glasses and enamels.

Isotopes of Erbium

Erbium has several stable isotopes, with Erbium-166 being the most abundant. These isotopes are used in various industrial and research applications.

Chemical Properties

Erbium typically forms compounds in the +3 oxidation state, such as Erbium(III) oxide. It reacts slowly with oxygen and water but forms stable oxides and salts.
