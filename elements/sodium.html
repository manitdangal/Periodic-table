Sodium

Sodium, symbol Na, is the eleventh element in the periodic table with an atomic number of 11. It is a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive metal. Sodium is an alkali metal and is known for its vigorous reactions with water and its presence in table salt (NaCl).

General Properties

  • Symbol: Na
  • Atomic Number: 11
  • Atomic Mass: 22.990 amu
  • Density: 0.968 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 97.72°C
  • Boiling Point: 883°C

Importance in the Universe

Sodium is the sixth most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is essential in biological processes and plays a key role in cellular functions, particularly in nerve transmission and muscle contraction. Sodium is also a critical component of sea salt, which covers much of Earth's surface.

Key Applications

Sodium has a wide range of applications in various industries:

  • Table Salt (NaCl): Sodium chloride, commonly known as table salt, is used in food preservation, seasoning, and industrial processes.
  • Soap Production: Sodium hydroxide (NaOH) is used in the production of soap through saponification.
  • Metal Production: Sodium vapor lamps are used for street lighting, and sodium is used in the extraction of other metals like titanium.
  • Heat Transfer: Sodium is used in some types of nuclear reactors as a coolant due to its high thermal conductivity.

Did You Know?

Sodium reacts explosively with water, producing hydrogen gas and heat. This reaction can be dangerous, which is why sodium must be stored under oil to prevent contact with moisture.

Chemical Properties

Sodium is an alkali metal, meaning it is highly reactive, especially with water. Some of its key chemical properties include:

  • Reactivity: Sodium reacts violently with water, producing sodium hydroxide (NaOH) and hydrogen gas (H₂), which can cause explosions.
  • Corrosive: Sodium hydroxide, a compound formed from sodium, is a strong base and can cause severe chemical burns.
  • Formation of Compounds: Sodium forms numerous compounds, including salts like sodium chloride (NaCl), which is vital for life, and sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO₃), commonly known as baking soda.
