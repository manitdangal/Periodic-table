Samarium

Samarium, symbol Sm, is a rare-earth metal with atomic number 62. It is a silvery-white metal that is relatively stable in air and is used in various applications, including in the manufacturing of magnets and other high-strength materials.

General Properties

  • Symbol: Sm
  • Atomic Number: 62
  • Atomic Mass: 150.36
  • Density: 7.52 g/cm³
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Melting Point: 1,070 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1,800 °C

Importance in the Universe

Samarium is an important rare-earth element that is found in trace amounts in the Earth's crust. It is significant for its magnetic properties and its use in the creation of high-strength magnets and in nuclear applications.

Key Applications

Samarium is used in a variety of applications, including:

  • Magnets: Samarium-cobalt magnets are used in electric motors, microphones, and other high-performance devices.
  • Alloys: Samarium is used in alloys that are important for high-temperature applications.
  • Nuclear Reactors: Samarium-149 is used as a neutron absorber in nuclear reactors.

Did You Know?

Samarium-cobalt magnets are among the strongest types of permanent magnets and are used in motors and sensors.

Isotopes of Samarium

Samarium has several isotopes, including Samarium-144 and Samarium-147. Samarium-147 is used in dating geological samples.

Chemical Properties

Samarium exhibits a +3 oxidation state in most of its compounds, which include Samarium(III) oxide and Samarium(III) chloride, both of which are important for its use in industry and research.
