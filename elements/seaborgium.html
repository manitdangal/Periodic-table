Seaborgium

Seaborgium, symbol Sg, is a synthetic element with atomic number 106. It was first synthesized in 1974 at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory in California, USA, by a team of scientists. The element is named after Glenn T. Seaborg, a famous American chemist who contributed greatly to the discovery of transuranium elements and the actinide series.
